# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Musicians — primarily string players and students — who have sheet music written
for one instrument/clef (e.g. violin in treble) and need it playable on another
(e.g. cello in bass). They use Clef Shift at a desk while practicing or arranging,
often with the original score in hand. The job: upload a score, get the notes
read and transposed into a cello-friendly version they can read and export.

## Product Purpose

Clef Shift reads notation directly from an uploaded score image/PDF, converts it
to a target-clef arrangement, and regenerates clean notation plus MusicXML.
Success is an honest, accurate read of the original notes and a usable converted
score — never a faked conversion.

## Brand Personality

Elegant, classical, conservatory. Calm and premium, like fine engraved sheet
music on cream paper. Quietly confident; the craft shows in restraint and detail,
not noise. Three words: refined, musical, trustworthy.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS dashboards (hero-metric templates, identical card grids, neon-on-navy
gradients). Loud "AI tool" aesthetics. Cluttered notation editors. Anything that
feels like a web form bolted onto a converter.

## Design Principles

- **Honest output** — never present the uploaded image or example data as if it
  were converted output; show failure plainly when detection fails.
- **Engraved calm** — the interface should feel like quality sheet music: warm
  paper, classical type, generous space, ink-dark marks.
- **Motion as phrasing** — animation is subtle and musical: gentle fades and
  staggered reveals, never distracting from the score.
- **Legibility first** — notation, note labels, and status must always be clearly
  readable; contrast is non-negotiable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Full `prefers-reduced-motion`
support (animations degrade to instant/crossfade). Status and error messages are
conveyed in text, not color alone.
